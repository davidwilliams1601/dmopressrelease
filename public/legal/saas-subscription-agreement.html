SaaS Subscription Agreement

PressPilot — Press Release Management Platform

This SaaS Subscription Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the date of the last signature below ("Effective Date") by and between:

[Your Company Legal Name], a company registered in England and Wales under company number [NUMBER], whose registered office is at [ADDRESS] ("Provider", "we", "us"); and

[Customer Organisation Name], of [ADDRESS] ("Customer", "you").

Together referred to as the "Parties" and each a "Party".

1. Definitions and Interpretation

"Authorised Users"
The Customer's employees, agents, and contractors who are authorised to use the Platform under this Agreement, up to the limits specified in the applicable Subscription Tier.
"Confidential Information"
Any information disclosed by one Party to the other that is marked as confidential or which ought reasonably to be considered confidential given its nature and the circumstances of disclosure.
"Customer Data"
All data, content, and materials uploaded to, stored on, or processed through the Platform by or on behalf of the Customer, including press releases, media contact lists, partner submissions, images, and analytics data.
"Data Processing Agreement" or "DPA"
The data processing terms set out in Schedule 2 of this Agreement, which form part of this Agreement.
"Effective Date"
The date on which this Agreement comes into force, as stated above.
"Initial Term"
The period of twelve (12) months from the Subscription Start Date.
"Intellectual Property Rights"
Patents, rights to inventions, copyright and related rights, trade marks, business names, domain names, rights in get-up and trade dress, goodwill and the right to sue for passing off, rights in designs, database rights, rights in computer software, and all other intellectual property rights, whether registered or unregistered.
"Partner Accounts"
User accounts created for third parties (such as tourism partners, members, or stakeholders) who submit content through the Partner Portal.
"Platform"
The PressPilot web-based software application and all associated services, APIs, and documentation provided by the Provider.
"Renewal Term"
Each subsequent twelve (12) month period following the Initial Term or a prior Renewal Term.
"Subscription Fees"
The fees payable by the Customer as set out in Schedule 1.
"Subscription Start Date"
The date on which the Customer's access to the Platform is activated following receipt of payment.
"Subscription Tier"
The level of service selected by the Customer (Starter, Professional, or Organisation), as described in Schedule 1.

2. Platform Access and Subscription

2.1 Grant of Access

Subject to the Customer's payment of the Subscription Fees and compliance with this Agreement, the Provider grants the Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable right to access and use the Platform during the Subscription Term for the Customer's internal business purposes.

2.2 Subscription Activation

The Customer's access to the Platform shall be activated upon receipt of payment of the applicable Subscription Fees. Until payment is received, the Customer's account shall remain in a pending state and access to the Platform's features shall be restricted.

2.3 Authorised Users

The Customer shall ensure that the number of Authorised Users and Partner Accounts does not exceed the limits specified for the applicable Subscription Tier. The Customer is responsible for all activities conducted through its user accounts.

2.4 Acceptable Use

The Customer shall not:

  1. use the Platform to send unsolicited bulk email (spam) or in violation of applicable anti-spam legislation;
  2. upload or distribute content that is unlawful, defamatory, threatening, or infringes any third party's rights;
  3. attempt to gain unauthorised access to the Platform or its underlying systems;
  4. reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble any part of the Platform;
  5. resell, sublicense, or make the Platform available to third parties other than Authorised Users and Partner Account holders;
  6. use the Platform in any way that could damage, disable, or impair the Platform or interfere with any other party's use.

3. Subscription Fees and Payment

3.1 Fees

The Customer shall pay the Subscription Fees as set out in Schedule 1. All fees are stated exclusive of VAT, which shall be added where applicable at the prevailing rate.

3.2 Invoicing

The Provider shall issue an invoice to the Customer for the Subscription Fees at the start of the Initial Term and at the start of each Renewal Term. Invoices for additional Partner Accounts shall be issued at the point of activation or at the next renewal, at the Provider's discretion.

3.3 Payment Terms

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all invoices are payable within fourteen (14) days of the invoice date. Payment may be made by bank transfer or via the Provider's online payment portal.

3.4 Late Payment

Without prejudice to any other right or remedy, if the Customer fails to make any payment when due, the Provider may:

  1. charge interest on the overdue amount at the rate of 4% per annum above the Bank of England base rate, accruing daily from the due date until payment is made;
  2. suspend access to the Platform until all outstanding amounts have been paid in full.

3.5 Fee Changes

The Provider may increase the Subscription Fees at each renewal by giving the Customer not less than sixty (60) days' written notice prior to the start of the relevant Renewal Term. If the Customer does not agree to the revised fees, it may terminate this Agreement in accordance with Clause 8.

4. Customer Data

4.1 Ownership

The Customer retains all right, title, and interest in and to the Customer Data. Nothing in this Agreement transfers ownership of Customer Data to the Provider.

4.2 Licence to Provider

The Customer grants the Provider a non-exclusive, worldwide licence to use, copy, store, and process the Customer Data solely for the purposes of providing and improving the Platform and performing the Provider's obligations under this Agreement.

4.3 Data Protection

Each Party shall comply with its obligations under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. The data processing terms set out in Schedule 2 shall apply to any processing of personal data by the Provider on behalf of the Customer.

4.4 Data Backup

The Provider shall maintain reasonable backup procedures for Customer Data. However, the Customer is responsible for maintaining its own backups of critical data.

4.5 Data Portability

Upon written request (and subject to payment of any outstanding fees), the Provider shall make Customer Data available for export in a commonly used, machine-readable format within thirty (30) days of such request.

5. Service Levels and Support

5.1 Availability

The Provider shall use commercially reasonable endeavours to make the Platform available 99.5% of the time in any calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance. Scheduled maintenance windows shall be communicated to the Customer with reasonable advance notice where practicable.

5.2 Support

The Provider shall provide email-based support during UK business hours (09:00–17:30, Monday to Friday, excluding English public holidays). Customers on the Organisation Tier shall receive priority support and a named account manager as further described in Schedule 1.

5.3 Updates and Changes

The Provider may update, modify, or enhance the Platform from time to time. The Provider shall use reasonable endeavours to ensure that material changes do not adversely affect the Customer's existing use of the Platform. The Provider shall notify the Customer of any material changes in advance.

6. Intellectual Property

6.1 Provider IP

The Platform, including all software, documentation, designs, algorithms, and related Intellectual Property Rights, is and shall remain the exclusive property of the Provider (or its licensors). This Agreement does not grant the Customer any rights in or to the Platform except as expressly set out herein.

6.2 Customer IP

The Customer retains all Intellectual Property Rights in the Customer Data and any materials provided by the Customer for use with the Platform (such as logos, branding assets, and press release content).

6.3 Feedback

If the Customer provides suggestions, ideas, or feedback regarding the Platform, the Provider may use such feedback without restriction or obligation to the Customer.

7. Confidentiality

7.1 Obligations

Each Party shall keep the other Party's Confidential Information strictly confidential and shall not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party, except:

  1. to its employees, officers, advisers, or subcontractors who need to know for the purposes of this Agreement, provided they are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations;
  2. as required by law, regulation, or order of a court or governmental authority.

7.2 Duration

The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

8. Term and Termination

8.1 Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for the Initial Term. Thereafter, it shall automatically renew for successive Renewal Terms unless either Party gives the other not less than sixty (60) days' written notice of non-renewal prior to the end of the then-current term.

8.2 Termination for Breach

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if:

  1. the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and (where the breach is capable of remedy) fails to remedy it within thirty (30) days of receiving written notice specifying the breach; or
  2. the other Party becomes insolvent, enters administration, or has a receiver or administrator appointed.

8.3 Termination by Customer

The Customer may terminate this Agreement at the end of any term by providing sixty (60) days' written notice. No refund of Subscription Fees already paid shall be due for early termination unless the Provider is in material breach.

8.4 Effects of Termination

Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement:

  1. the Customer's access to the Platform shall cease;
  2. the Provider shall, at the Customer's written request made within thirty (30) days of termination, make Customer Data available for export in accordance with Clause 4.5;
  3. following the thirty (30) day period referred to above (or earlier export), the Provider may delete all Customer Data from its systems, subject to any legal retention requirements;
  4. all provisions of this Agreement that are expressed or intended to survive termination shall continue in force.

9. Limitation of Liability

9.1 Exclusion of Certain Losses

Neither Party shall be liable to the other for any indirect, special, incidental, or consequential loss or damage, including (without limitation) loss of profits, revenue, business, goodwill, or anticipated savings, howsoever arising, whether in contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty, or otherwise.

9.2 Liability Cap

Subject to Clause 9.3, the Provider's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed an amount equal to the Subscription Fees paid by the Customer in the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

9.3 Unlimited Liability

Nothing in this Agreement shall limit or exclude either Party's liability for:

  1. death or personal injury caused by its negligence;
  2. fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
  3. any liability which cannot be limited or excluded by applicable law.

10. Warranties and Disclaimers

10.1 Provider Warranties

The Provider warrants that:

  1. it has the right and authority to enter into this Agreement and provide the Platform;
  2. the Platform shall be provided with reasonable care and skill;
  3. it shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations in performing its obligations under this Agreement.

10.2 Disclaimer

Except as expressly stated in this Agreement, the Platform is provided "as is". The Provider makes no warranties, whether express, implied, statutory, or otherwise, including any implied warranties of merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, or non-infringement, to the maximum extent permitted by law.

10.3 AI-Generated Content

The Platform includes artificial intelligence features that generate suggested content (including press release text, headlines, and social media posts). Such AI-generated content is provided as a drafting aid only. The Customer is solely responsible for reviewing, editing, and approving all content before publication or distribution.

11. General Provisions

11.1 Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations where such failure or delay results from circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including (without limitation) acts of God, fire, flood, pandemic, government action, or failure of third-party telecommunications or hosting services.

11.2 Assignment

The Customer shall not assign or transfer this Agreement without the Provider's prior written consent. The Provider may assign this Agreement to any successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets.

11.3 Notices

All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and sent to the email addresses specified in the Order Form or as subsequently notified in writing. Notices shall be deemed received on the next business day after sending.

11.4 Entire Agreement

This Agreement (including its Schedules) constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior discussions, negotiations, and agreements relating to its subject matter.

11.5 Amendments

No amendment to this Agreement shall be effective unless it is in writing and signed by or on behalf of both Parties.

11.6 Waiver

No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right or remedy shall constitute a waiver of that right or remedy.

11.7 Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, that provision shall be deemed modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

11.8 Third Party Rights

No person who is not a Party to this Agreement shall have any right under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 to enforce any term of this Agreement.

11.9 Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales shall have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement.

Schedule 2 — Data Processing Agreement

1. Scope and Roles

For the purposes of UK GDPR, the Customer is the Data Controller and the Provider is the Data Processor in respect of personal data processed through the Platform on the Customer's behalf.

2. Categories of Personal Data

The personal data processed under this Agreement may include:

  • Media contact details (names, email addresses, job titles, publication names)
  • Partner/member details (names, email addresses, business descriptions, social media handles)
  • Customer team member details (names, email addresses)
  • Email engagement data (open/click events, IP addresses, user agents)
  • Media request details (journalist names, email addresses, story requests)

3. Data Subjects

Data subjects include the Customer's media contacts, partners/members, team members, and journalists submitting media requests.

4. Purpose and Duration

Personal data shall be processed solely for the purpose of providing the Platform services described in this Agreement, for the duration of this Agreement plus any retention period specified in Clause 8.4.

5. Provider Obligations

The Provider shall:

  1. process personal data only on the Customer's documented instructions, unless required to do so by applicable law;
  2. ensure that persons authorised to process personal data are bound by appropriate obligations of confidentiality;
  3. implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest;
  4. not engage a sub-processor without prior written authorisation of the Customer (general authorisation is granted for the sub-processors listed in paragraph 7 below, subject to the Provider notifying the Customer of any changes);
  5. assist the Customer in responding to data subject access requests and other data protection rights requests;
  6. assist the Customer in meeting its obligations regarding data breach notification, data protection impact assessments, and consultation with supervisory authorities;
  7. notify the Customer without undue delay (and in any event within 48 hours) upon becoming aware of a personal data breach;
  8. at the Customer's choice, delete or return all personal data upon termination of this Agreement, and delete existing copies unless required by law to retain them;
  9. make available to the Customer all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with these obligations.

6. International Transfers

The Provider shall not transfer personal data outside the United Kingdom without appropriate safeguards in place, including (where applicable) Standard Contractual Clauses or reliance on an adequacy decision.
