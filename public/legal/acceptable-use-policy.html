Acceptable Use Policy

PressPilot — Last updated: [DATE]

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") sets out the rules that apply to all use of the PressPilot platform ("Platform") by organisations, their team members, partners, and any other authorised users. This AUP forms part of the SaaS Subscription Agreement between you and [Your Company Legal Name] ("Provider", "we", "us").

By using the Platform, you agree to comply with this AUP. We may update this AUP from time to time, and continued use of the Platform constitutes acceptance of any changes.

1. Email Distribution Standards

The Platform enables organisations to distribute press releases and communications to media contacts. When using these features, you must:

  1. Only email legitimate media contacts — recipients must be journalists, editors, bloggers, or media professionals with a reasonable expectation of receiving press communications relevant to their area of coverage.
  2. Maintain accurate and up-to-date contact lists — promptly remove bounced addresses, unsubscribe requests, and contacts who have asked not to be contacted.
  3. Honour unsubscribe requests — if a recipient asks to stop receiving communications (whether by reply, unsubscribe link, or other means), you must remove them from your lists within 5 working days.
  4. Not purchase or scrape email lists — all media contacts must be obtained through legitimate professional relationships, publicly available press contact information, or direct enquiry.
  5. Not send unsolicited bulk email (spam) — the Platform is designed for targeted press distribution, not mass marketing.
  6. Comply with applicable laws — including the UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR), UK GDPR, and any other applicable anti-spam or marketing legislation.

2. Content Standards

All content created, uploaded, or distributed through the Platform must be:

  1. Lawful — must not violate any applicable law, regulation, or court order.
  2. Truthful and not misleading — press releases and public communications should be factually accurate. While the Platform provides AI-assisted drafting tools, you are solely responsible for the accuracy and truthfulness of all published content.
  3. Non-infringing — must not infringe any third party's intellectual property rights, including copyright, trade marks, or moral rights. You must have the right to use any images, text, or materials you upload.

Content must not:

  • Be defamatory, libellous, or maliciously false
  • Be obscene, offensive, hateful, or discriminatory
  • Promote illegal activities or violence
  • Contain malware, viruses, or malicious code
  • Impersonate any person or organisation
  • Include personal data of individuals without a lawful basis
  • Violate any person's right to privacy

3. Partner Portal Usage

When inviting partners, members, or third parties to submit content through the Partner Portal:

  1. You must ensure that partners understand and consent to how their submitted content may be used (including incorporation into press releases and web content).
  2. You are responsible for the accuracy of consent mechanisms and for maintaining records of consent.
  3. You must not use the Partner Portal to collect personal data beyond what is necessary for the stated purpose.
  4. Partner accounts must only be created for legitimate business partners, members, or stakeholders of your organisation.

4. AI-Generated Content

The Platform includes artificial intelligence features for content generation, including press release drafting, headline suggestions, tone enhancement, social media posts, and theme analysis. When using these features:

  1. You are responsible for all output — AI-generated content is a starting point. You must review, verify, and approve all content before publication or distribution.
  2. Check factual accuracy — AI models may produce inaccurate or misleading statements. Verify all facts, dates, names, statistics, and claims.
  3. Do not rely on AI for legal, medical, or financial statements — seek appropriate professional advice for specialised content.
  4. Attribution — you are not required to disclose that content was AI-assisted, but you may do so if you choose.

5. Account Security

You are responsible for maintaining the security of your account:

  1. Use strong, unique passwords for all user accounts.
  2. Do not share login credentials between individuals — each user must have their own account.
  3. Promptly revoke access for team members or partners who leave your organisation or no longer require access.
  4. Notify us immediately at david@press-pilot.com if you suspect unauthorised access to your account.
  5. You are responsible for all activities that occur under your organisation's accounts.

6. System Integrity

You must not:

  1. Attempt to gain unauthorised access to the Platform, other users' accounts, or any systems or networks connected to the Platform.
  2. Use automated scripts, bots, or scrapers to access the Platform (except through our documented APIs, if provided).
  3. Intentionally overload, disrupt, or interfere with the Platform's infrastructure or performance.
  4. Reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code of the Platform.
  5. Circumvent any security measures, rate limits, or access controls.
  6. Use the Platform to benchmark, build, or support a competing product or service.

7. Sending Limits and Fair Use

The Platform is designed for press release distribution, not high-volume transactional or marketing email. We reserve the right to apply reasonable sending limits to maintain deliverability and platform performance for all customers. Current fair use guidelines:

  • Email distribution is intended for press releases and targeted media communications.
  • Partner communications should be relevant, purposeful, and not excessive in frequency.
  • If your usage significantly exceeds normal patterns, we may contact you to discuss your requirements.

8. White Label and Branding

If your subscription includes white-label or custom branding features:

  1. You may only use branding assets (logos, colours, names) that you have the legal right to use.
  2. You must not use branding features to impersonate another organisation or create a misleading impression of the origin of communications.
  3. Where "Powered by PressPilot" attribution is included in your subscription tier, you must not remove, hide, or obscure this attribution.

9. Enforcement

If we reasonably believe that you have violated this AUP, we may:

  1. Issue a warning — notify you of the violation and request corrective action.
  2. Suspend access — temporarily restrict access to all or part of the Platform while the issue is investigated. We will notify you of any suspension and the reasons for it.
  3. Remove content — remove or disable access to content that violates this AUP.
  4. Terminate the Agreement — in cases of serious or repeated violations, terminate your subscription in accordance with the SaaS Subscription Agreement.

We will endeavour to give reasonable notice before taking enforcement action, except where immediate action is necessary to protect the Platform, other users, or third parties, or to comply with legal requirements.

10. Reporting Violations

If you become aware of any violation of this AUP — whether by users within your organisation, your partners, or any other party — please notify us promptly at david@press-pilot.com.

If you are a media contact who has received unwanted communications through our Platform, please contact us at david@press-pilot.com and we will investigate and take appropriate action.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this AUP from time to time. We will notify you of material changes via email or an in-app notification at least fourteen (14) days before the changes take effect. Continued use of the Platform after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated AUP.
