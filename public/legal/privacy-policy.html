1. Introduction

[Your Company Legal Name] ("we", "us", "our") operates the PressPilot platform ("Platform"), a press release management service for organisations. We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal data.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and share personal data when you:

  • visit our website at press-pilot.com;
  • use the PressPilot platform as an organisation administrator or team member;
  • use the Partner Portal to submit content;
  • receive emails distributed through the Platform;
  • submit a media enquiry through a public form; or
  • otherwise interact with us.

We are the data controller for personal data collected directly by us (e.g. account registration, website visitors). When organisations use the Platform to manage their own contacts and distribute emails, we act as a data processor on their behalf — see Section 9 for details.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account and Organisation Data

When an organisation subscribes to PressPilot, we collect:

  • Organisation name, address, and billing contact details
  • Administrator and team member names and email addresses
  • Login credentials (email and hashed password — we never store plain-text passwords)
  • Organisation branding assets (logos, brand colours)
  • Press contact details published on public pages

2.2 Partner Portal Data

When partners or members register and submit content, we collect:

  • Name and email address
  • Business description and categories
  • Social media handles (optional)
  • Submitted content (text, images)
  • Profile avatar (optional)

2.3 Media Contact Data (Processed on Behalf of Organisations)

Organisations upload and manage their own media contact lists. This data may include:

  • Journalist names, email addresses, job titles, and publication names

We process this data solely on the organisation's instructions as a data processor. The organisation is the data controller for this data.

2.4 Email Engagement Data

When press releases are distributed via the Platform, we collect:

  • Email delivery status (delivered, bounced, failed)
  • Open and click events (via tracking pixels and link redirects)
  • Approximate timestamp, IP address, and user agent of engagement events

This data is processed on behalf of the organisation distributing the email.

2.5 Media Enquiry Data

When journalists submit enquiries through public forms, we collect:

  • Name, email address, outlet/publication name
  • Story topic, destinations of interest, deadline
  • Any additional information provided

2.6 Technical and Usage Data

We automatically collect certain data when you use the Platform or visit our website:

  • IP address and approximate location
  • Browser type and version, operating system
  • Pages visited, features used, and time spent
  • Referring URL

We use Google Analytics for website analytics. See Section 7 for cookie details.

2.7 Payment Data

Payment information (card details, bank account numbers) is collected and processed directly by our payment processor, Stripe. We do not store payment card details on our systems. We retain invoice records and transaction references.

8. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure — request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Right to restrict processing — request that we limit how we use your data
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at david@press-pilot.com. We will respond within one month of receiving your request, as required by law.

Media contacts receiving press releases: If you receive emails distributed by an organisation through PressPilot and wish to exercise your data protection rights, please contact the sending organisation directly, as they are the data controller for your data. You may also contact us and we will direct your request to the appropriate organisation.

9. Our Role as Data Processor

When organisations use PressPilot to manage media contacts, distribute press releases, and collect partner submissions, we act as a data processor under UK GDPR. The organisation is the data controller and determines the purposes and means of processing.

Our processing activities as a data processor are governed by the Data Processing Agreement included in our SaaS Subscription Agreement. We process personal data only on the organisation's documented instructions.

If you have questions about how an organisation uses your data through PressPilot, please contact that organisation directly.

10. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS/HTTPS) for all data transmitted to and from the Platform
  • Encryption at rest for stored data (provided by Google Cloud/Firebase)
  • Role-based access controls and multi-tenant data isolation
  • Firebase Authentication with secure password hashing
  • Firestore security rules enforcing per-organisation data boundaries
  • Regular security reviews of application code and infrastructure
  • Access to production systems restricted to authorised personnel

11. Children's Data

The Platform is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If we become aware that we have collected data from a child, we will take steps to delete it promptly.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. We will notify Platform users of material changes via email or an in-app notification. The "Last updated" date at the top of this policy indicates when it was most recently revised.
