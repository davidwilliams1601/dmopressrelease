Demo walkthrough
Step-by-step — what to show & what to say
Use the Visit Cornish Riviera demo account. Log in at press-pilot.com with the credentials your account manager has provided.
1
The Dashboard — Command Centre
What to show
  • The main dashboard overview
  • Stats at a glance (partners, releases, emails sent)
  • Recent activity feed
What to say
"This is the command centre — everything in one place. At a glance you can see how many partners are active, how many releases have gone out, and how your latest campaign performed."
2
The Partner Portal — Collecting Member Stories
What to show
  • The Partners section — list of members
  • Open the partner-facing portal in a new tab
  • Show the submission form a partner sees
What to say
"Your members get their own portal — they log in, fill in a simple form about their news, and it lands straight in your inbox. No emails, no spreadsheets, no chasing."
Tip: Ask the prospect — "How do your members currently send you their news?" This almost always opens up a pain point conversation.
3
Partner Submissions — Stories Ready to Use
What to show
  • The Submissions inbox
  • Open an existing submission
  • Show the content, category tags and images
What to say
"Once a partner submits, it appears here — structured, categorised, ready to act on. You can review it, approve it for a release, or request more detail. Nothing gets lost."
4
AI Press Release Drafting — The Time Saver
What to show
  • Open the Releases section
  • Open an existing drafted release
  • Show the AI-generated draft with brand tone
  • Highlight the edit and publish controls
What to say
"The AI takes the submission and produces a full press release — in your tone of voice, with your boilerplate, ready to send. You review it, tweak anything you want, and it's done. What used to take half a day now takes minutes."
Tip: This is usually the biggest reaction moment. Pause here and let them absorb it. Ask: "How long does it currently take your team to draft a release?"
5
Outlet Lists & Sending — Right Story, Right Desk
What to show
  • The Outlets section — journalist lists
  • Show how lists are organised (regional, trade, national)
  • Show the send flow from a release
What to say
"Your journalist contacts live here — organised into lists however makes sense for you. When you're ready to send, you choose your list, review the send, and that's it. One click."
Tip: If they say their contacts are in a spreadsheet, reassure them — "We can import that in minutes. You don't lose anything you've already built."
6
Analytics — Prove the Value
What to show
  • Open a sent release's analytics view
  • Show open rate, click rate, delivery stats
  • Show the per-journalist breakdown
What to say
"After every send, you can see exactly who opened it, who clicked through, and who engaged. For the first time, you have data to take back to your board and your members — not just 'we sent 40 releases' but 'here's who noticed.'"
Tip: Close the loop here — "This data also helps your members feel the value. They can see their story actually reached journalists." Members feeling valued reduces churn.

Handling objections
Common questions and how to respond
Stay confident and curious — most objections are really just requests for more information.
We already manage this in email and Word
Your responsePressPilot doesn't replace your process — it makes it structured and measurable. You keep full editorial control, but the AI does the drafting and the data does the proving. Most teams see the difference within the first release.
Our journalist contacts are in a spreadsheet
Your responsePerfect — that's exactly what we'd import on day one. You don't lose what you've already built. We just make it easier to use, segment and track.
Our members won't bother using a portal
Your responseThe portal is designed to be as simple as a contact form — it takes under two minutes to submit. And because members can see their stories going out, they tend to engage more, not less.
We don't have the budget right now
Your responseUnderstood — worth asking though: how many hours a week does your team spend drafting releases and chasing member content? Most organisations find PressPilot pays for itself within the first month of use.
Can the AI really write in our tone?
Your responseYes — during setup we configure your brand tone, boilerplate and style guidelines. The AI uses these every time. You'll still review and edit, but the starting point is already on-brand.
