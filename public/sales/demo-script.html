PressPilot Sales Demo Script

Internal use only — 30-minute guided walkthrough

Pre-Demo Preparation

  • Log into the demo account and check it's populated with sample data
  • Have at least 2-3 sample releases (one Draft, one Sent with analytics)
  • Have at least 1 partner submission in the system
  • Have an outlet list with test recipients
  • Know the prospect's organisation name, sector, and current PR process
  • Have the public newsroom URL ready to show
  • Clear any browser notifications from previous demos
  • Open the partner portal in a separate browser tab (incognito) ready to show

Key Discovery Questions (ask before or at the start)

  1. How do you currently create and distribute press releases?
  2. How many press releases do you send per month/quarter?
  3. Do your members/partners submit content to you? How?
  4. How do you report on PR activity to your board/stakeholders?
  5. How many people are involved in your PR/comms process?

0:00 Opening — Set the Scene

"Thanks for your time today. I'm going to walk you through PressPilot — it's a platform we've built specifically for organisations like yours that need to collect content from partners, turn it into professional press releases, and get it in front of the right journalists. The whole process — from partner submission to published story — happens in one place. Let me show you how."
Keep the opening under 2 minutes. The platform sells itself — get into the product quickly.

2:00 Stop 1 — Dashboard Overview

Navigate to the Dashboard. Point out the stat cards and the AI Insights panel.
"This is your command centre. At a glance you can see how many releases you've sent, your open rates, and page views. The AI Insights panel analyses your recent activity and gives you strategic recommendations — things like which audience segments are engaging most, or what day of the week gets the best open rates."
Instant visibility into PR performance without spreadsheets. The AI does the analysis for you.
If the demo account is fresh, mention the Getting Started checklist: "When a new org signs up, they get a guided setup — 10 steps that walk them through everything."

4:00 Stop 2 — Partner Submission Portal

"Let me start with where the content comes from. One of the biggest challenges we hear is collecting stories from partners. Chasing emails, formatting content, following up — it's a huge time sink. PressPilot solves this."
Switch to the Partner Portal tab (incognito browser). Show the submission form.
"Each partner gets their own login to a branded self-service portal. They fill in their story, upload images, tag the content — and it lands directly in your dashboard, ready for review. No more email chains."
Show the tag selector and image upload. Mention the social handles capture.
"We also capture their social media handles so when you feature their story, you've got everything you need for cross-promotion."
Eliminates the biggest bottleneck in member PR — collecting content. Partners do the work, your team focuses on editorial.
"Our partners won't use another system."
Response: "The portal is deliberately simple — a title, their story, and images. There's no training needed. And because it's branded with your logo and colours, it feels like your system, not a third party tool."

8:00 Stop 3 — Submissions Dashboard & AI Analysis

Switch back to the admin dashboard. Navigate to Submissions. Show the submission list with AI themes.
"Every submission is automatically analysed by AI when it arrives. It identifies the key themes — things like 'Cultural Heritage', 'Adventure Tourism', 'Food & Drink' — so you can quickly filter and find the right content for your next release."
Click into a submission. Show the AI themes, the partner details, and the status workflow.
"You review each submission, add internal notes, and track it through your workflow — submitted, reviewed, used in a release, or archived. Nothing falls through the cracks."
AI categorisation replaces manual reading and sorting. Your team spends time on editorial decisions, not admin.

11:00 Stop 4 — Creating a Press Release with AI

This is the "wow" moment — take your time here.

Go back to Submissions. Select 1-2 submissions using checkboxes. Click "Generate Draft".
"Now here's where it gets exciting. I've selected two partner submissions. With one click, PressPilot's AI takes that raw content and generates a publication-ready press release."
Walk through the generation dialog. Show the AI producing a headline, body copy, suggested campaign type and audience. Pause to let them read it.
"It's drafted the headline, structured the body copy, and suggested the campaign type and audience. All based on the actual partner content and your organisation's brand voice. You can edit anything before accepting."
Accept the draft. Navigate to the release editor. Show the full editing experience.
"And now we're in the editor. Let me show you the AI tools built in here..."
Click "Generate Headlines" — show 3 options appearing. Pick one.
"Three headline options in seconds. Pick the one that works, or tweak it."
Click "Enhance Tone" — show the AI refining the copy.
"The tone enhancer takes your draft and aligns it to your brand voice. If you've got brand guidelines in your settings — formal, conversational, whatever — it respects those."
Click "Generate Social Posts". Show all 4 platform tabs with character counts.
"And with one more click — social media posts for LinkedIn, X, Facebook, and Instagram. Each one formatted for the platform, with the right hashtag count and character limits. Copy and paste straight into your scheduling tool."
What used to take your team half a day — drafting a release, writing social posts, checking tone — takes minutes. And the quality is consistent every time.
"We'd want to edit everything before it goes out."
Response: "Absolutely — that's the design. The AI gives you an 80% first draft. Your editorial team adds the final 20% — the local knowledge, the quotes, the nuance. It's a drafting assistant, not an autopilot."

16:00 Stop 5 — Distribution & Tracking

Open the Send Release dialog. Show outlet list selection and recipient count.
"When the release is ready, you choose which outlet lists to send to. You can build multiple lists — local press, trade press, nationals, broadcast — and target each release to the right audience."
Show the scheduled send option with the date/time picker.
"You can send immediately or schedule for a specific date and time — useful for embargoed releases or timing around events."
Navigate to a previously sent release. Show the analytics panel.
"Once it's sent, you get real-time tracking. Opens, clicks, bounces — you can see exactly which journalists are engaging with your content. This chart shows engagement over time."
No more sending press releases into a black hole. You know exactly who opened it, who clicked through, and who ignored it — so you can follow up intelligently.

19:00 Stop 6 — Public Newsroom & Release Pages

Open the public newsroom in a new tab. Show the branded page with releases listed.
"Every organisation gets a public newsroom — a professional press centre branded with your logo and colours. Journalists can browse your releases, and you can link to this from your website."
Click into an individual release page. Show the full public view.
"Each release gets its own shareable URL. Great for social media, for linking in pitch emails, or embedding on your own site."
Show the media enquiry form link at the bottom of the newsroom.
"And there's a built-in media enquiry form. Journalists can pitch story ideas directly to you — those land in your dashboard alongside everything else."
A professional public-facing press centre with zero web development. Live in minutes, branded to your organisation.

22:00 Stop 7 — Stakeholder Reporting

Navigate to Reports. Show the executive summary with stat cards and trend arrows.
"This is one of the features our customers love most. The Reports section gives you a comprehensive performance report you can take to your board or senior stakeholders."
Walk through: executive summary, engagement trends chart, top releases table, partner engagement section, audience breakdown.
"You can switch between monthly, quarterly, or custom date ranges. It shows everything — releases sent, journalist reach, open rates, partner contribution, top performing content."
Click "Export PDF". Show the print preview with the org logo and clean formatting.
"And with one click, export it as a PDF for your board pack or presentation. It includes your logo and the reporting period — ready to present."
No more scrambling to pull together PR reports before board meetings. The data is always there, always up to date, and presentation-ready in seconds.
"We already track this in spreadsheets."
Response: "The difference is this is automatic. Every email send, every open, every click — it's captured in real time. No manual data entry, no formula errors, and it's always current."

25:00 Stop 8 — Branding & Settings (quick)

Navigate to Settings. Scroll to the Branding card. Show the logo upload, colour picker, and preview.
"Everything is customisable to your brand. Upload your logo, set your brand colours — they apply automatically across your newsroom, emails to journalists, partner reports, and PDF exports."
If selling Professional or Organisation tier, mention: "On the Organisation plan, we remove all PressPilot branding entirely — it's fully white-labelled as your own platform."
Briefly show the approval workflow toggle and team management.
"You can also set up approval workflows — so releases go through an internal review before anyone can hit send. And manage your team with different permission levels."

27:00 Stop 9 — Partner Communications

Navigate to Settings > Partners. Show partner list and email compose.
"Finally — you can communicate directly with your partner network from here. Send targeted emails by category, share impact reports showing how their content performed, or send quarterly roundups."
"This is powerful for partner retention. You're showing them tangible results — 'Your story reached 500 journalists with a 42% open rate.' That's a compelling reason for them to keep contributing."
Closes the loop with partners. They see the impact of their contribution, which drives ongoing engagement and justifies their membership/partnership.

29:00 Close — Pricing & Next Steps

"So that's PressPilot. To recap — your partners submit content through a branded portal. Your team uses AI to turn it into press releases, social posts, and web content in minutes. You distribute to targeted journalist lists with real-time tracking. And you report the impact to stakeholders with a single click."

Common Objections & Responses

"We don't have enough partners to justify this."
Even with 3-4 active partners, you're saving significant time on content collection and press release production. The AI drafting alone pays for itself in team hours. And the platform grows with you — as you recruit more partners, you don't need more staff.
"We already use Mailchimp / email marketing tool for press releases."
Mailchimp is built for marketing emails, not press distribution. PressPilot gives you a journalist-specific workflow — outlet lists with press contacts, per-release engagement tracking, a public newsroom, and the AI drafting that turns partner content into press-ready copy. They're different tools for different jobs.
"The AI content won't be good enough for our brand."
Show them a generated release during the demo. Emphasise: the AI learns your brand tone from your settings, and everything is editable. It's a first draft, not the final word. Most teams find they edit about 20% — the structure, the format, the tone are already there.
"We need to involve our IT team / data protection officer."
We're UK GDPR compliant with a full Data Processing Agreement, documented sub-processors, and encryption in transit and at rest. Happy to share our privacy policy and DPA for review. The platform runs on Google Cloud/Firebase — enterprise-grade infrastructure.
"Can we trial it first?"
We can set up a demo environment with your branding so you can explore the platform with your team. [Discuss trial terms as appropriate.]
"We need custom domain / fully white-labelled."
That's our Organisation tier. Your newsroom, emails, and partner portal all appear as your own platform — no PressPilot branding anywhere. We can walk through the white-label options in a follow-up call.

  • Attach the SaaS Subscription Agreement for review
